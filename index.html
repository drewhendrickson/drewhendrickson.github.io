About me:

I am an Assistant Professor in the Cognitive Science and Artificial Intelligence department at Tilburg University in the Netherlands, as well as the Program Director of the Data Science and Soceity Masters Program.

My research focuses on using machine learning and statistical models to predict and understand how people learn, when they are feeling stressed, or when they are not healthy. Recently I've worked on projects that study how the environment shapes language learning (cognitive modeling), how phone use and momentary stress are related (machine learning), and how visual imagery might be a target for interventions to help people with bipolar disorder (statistical modeling).

I teach advanced python programming and supervise Data Science Master's students thesis projects.
